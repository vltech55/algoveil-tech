Sentinel — hours of interviews, minutes of insight.

A long-form interview intelligence platform for investigative analysts. Ingests hours of transcripts, surfaces cross-document patterns, extracts structured entities and claims, and keeps a human analyst firmly in the loop on every finding that ships into a case file.

Context

The client's analysts review long-form interview transcripts as part of investigative work. Individual interviews run one to three hours. A single case can involve twenty to eighty interviews. The manual first-pass review — reading every transcript, tagging entities, cross-referencing quotes — was consuming three to five analyst-days per case.

The bottleneck wasn't skill. It was the reading. They wanted a tool that could ingest the transcripts, surface the patterns that would justify a human read, and never take a finding to a case file without an analyst's explicit sign-off.

The problem

Three constraints shaped the design:

  • The analyst must own every finding. A model can propose; only a human can promote a proposal to a case-file assertion.
  • Every claim must be traceable to a source span. A quote in the case file must resolve back to a specific timestamp in a specific transcript, with the surrounding context preserved.
  • Cross-document reasoning must be first-class. The whole point is finding patterns across interviews that a single-transcript read would miss.
The value isn't summarization. It's directing an analyst's attention to the fifteen minutes of transcript that matter, out of the eighty hours they'd otherwise be reading. — From the engagement brief

Approach

Ingest, structure, cross-reference, and present — with the analyst holding the pen at every stage that materially affects the case file.

  • Ingest — transcripts are chunked by speaker turn, embedded, and indexed. Speaker-level metadata (identity, role, session) is preserved through the pipeline.
  • Extract — a structured-extraction pass identifies entities (people, places, events, dates, amounts), claims (assertions of fact by the speaker), and relations (who is connected to whom by what interaction).
  • Cross-reference — semantic search surfaces near-duplicate claims across interviews. Contradictions are flagged for analyst review, not resolved by the model.
  • Present — the analyst workspace shows proposed findings in a review queue with confidence scores and source spans. Every promotion to the case file requires a click and a comment.

Chunks are stored in Postgres with pgvector; the extract-and-flag pass runs as an async Celery task on ingest. Analysts see new findings on a real-time review board as they land — no page refresh, no batch delays.

Analyst review workflow

Every finding in the review queue has four possible actions:

  • Promote — the finding is correct and belongs in the case file. Requires a one-line justification.
  • Edit & promote — the finding is directionally right but the wording needs adjustment. Preserves the source span link.
  • Dismiss — not relevant to this case. Adds a training signal for the extraction model.
  • Escalate — this looks important but needs a second analyst. Assigns to a peer.

Every action is logged with analyst identity, timestamp, and reasoning. The case file that ships to court has a full audit trail from the exhibit-quality quote back to the original transcript timestamp back to the analyst who approved it.

Trust & audit trail

No model output touches the case file directly. The extraction pass emits proposals; the review queue records analyst decisions; the case file is assembled from approved decisions only. If a finding is later challenged, the audit log shows the exact source span, the analyst who approved it, and the model version that surfaced it — reproducible six months later.

Model versions are pinned per case. Upgrading the extraction model on an active case is a deliberate action that requires re-review of any propagated findings — not a silent version bump.

Outcome

Sentinel is in production. Analysts now spend their time reviewing the fifteen most-relevant minutes of transcript per case instead of reading all eighty hours. The case files carry a stronger audit trail than the manual process ever did — every quote is traceable, every approval is logged, every model version is pinned.

The client's engineering team took over operational ownership at close of engagement. Extraction-model updates now go through their own eval-and-promote pipeline.

The right measure of an analyst tool is whether the audit trail comes out stronger than the manual workflow it replaces — not just faster. — Algo Veil engagement standard · Analyst tooling
